     THIS IS A MODIFIED VERSION OF  JULIAN - THE HILLMAN-EYAL EVAPOR.
     MONTE-CARLO CODE COUPLING ANGULAR MOMENTUM. THIS VERSION IS CALLED

      P A C E   - PROJECTION ANGULAR-MOMENTUM COUPLED EVAPORATION.

     MANY NEW FEATURES HAVE BEEN ADDED AND A MAJOR PART OF THE CODE
     REWRITTEN. IT HAS BEEN MODIFIED TO HAVE CONVENIENT, SIMPLE
     INPUT AND TO RUN MUCH MORE EFFICIENTLY. IT CAN NOW HANDLE
     HIGH EXCITATION ENERGIES AND SPINS IN SHORT RUNNING TIMES, THUS
     ENABLING ITS USE FOR HIGH ENERGY HEAVY ION REACTIONS.


 1)   THE INPUT HAS BEEN SHORTENED. THERE ARE DEFAULT OPTIONS
      FOR ALMOST EVERYTHING THE PROGRAM NEEDS.

 2)   MASSES ARE READ OFF THE WAPSTRA-1977 MASS TABLE
      IF TABLE DOES NOT CONTAIN MASS, LIQUID DROP MASS (LYSEKIL-1966)
      IS SUBSTITUTED.

 3)   THE LIGHT EMITTED PARTICLES ALL HAVE DEFAULT OPTICAL MODEL
      PARAMETERS.

 4)   THE STORAGE OF TL'S HAS BEEN COMPLETELY REWRITTEN -
     THE SAME TL'S ARE USED FOR ALL COMPOUND NUCLEI.THE SHIFT IN
     THE COULOMB BARRIER DURING DEEXCITATION HAS BEEN ACCOUNTED FOR
     BY CALCULATING THE TL'S AT AN EFFECTIVE ENERGY DETERMINED BY
     THE SHIFT.

 5)  A DEFAULT LEVEL DENSITY IS TAKEN FROM GILBERT AND CAMERON-
     THE LITTLE-A PARAMETER CAN ALTERNATIVELY BE TAKEN AS A/CONST.
     THE PROGRAM DECIDES DURING DEEXCITATION WHAT LEVEL DENSITIES
     AND MASSES IT NEEDS, AND THIS DOES NOT HAVE TO BE DECIDED IN
     THE PREPARATION OF THE INPUT
 **** THE YRAST LINE OF GILBERT-CAMERON CAN BE REPLACED BY THE COHEN-
 **** PLASIL-SWIATECKI YRAST LINE. THE YRAST FUNCTION IS BY COURTESY
 **** OF DR. F. PLASIL, O.R.N.L.

 6)  FISSION IS ALSO CONSIDERED AS A DECAY  MODE, IF THE FISSION
     BARRIER AT THE ANGULAR MOMENTUM CONSIDERED IS LESS THAN 30 MEV
     THIS CUTOFF LEVEL CAN BE CHANGED IN THE PROGRAM.

     THE DEFAULT FISSION BARRIER IS THE COHEN-PLASIL-SWIATECKI
     ROTATING LIQUID DROP FISSION BARRIER. YOU ARE WELCOME, HOWEVER
     TO INPUT YOUR OWN BARRIER.  THE SADDLE POINT LEVEL DENSITY IS
     DETERMINED BY A LITTLE-A PARAMETER TIMES ARATIO.
     ARATIO IS ACTUALLY  'A-FISS' / 'A-GAMMA'
     ( SEE BELOW )

 7)  AN INTERNAL DISCRIMINATOR PREVENTS WASTING TIME CALCULATING
     PROBABILITIES FOR HIGHLY UNLIKELY EVENTS..



 8)  THE PROJECTION OF THE ANGULAR MOMENTUM IS ALSO FOLLOWED
     *******************************************************

     THE PURPOSE OF THIS IS TO OBTAIN CORRECT ANGULAR DISTRIBUTIONS
     OF THE RESIDUAL NUCLEI AND THE EMITTED PARTICLES. FRACTIONAL
     (I.E. SPIN 1/2 ) ANGULAR MOMENTUM IS NEGLECTED IN THIS PART
     ( AND IN THIS PART ONLY ) OF THE CALCULATIONS SINCE IT SHOULD
     NOT HAVE A SIGNIFICANT EFFECT ON THESE DISTRIBUTIONS.

     FOR COMPOUND NUCLEUS REACTIONS ( MDIR=0, SEE FOLLOWING) ANGULAR
     DISTIBUTIONS ARE DETERMINED AS FUNTION OF ANGLE AROUND RECOIL AXIS
     FOR DEEP INELASTIC PRODUCTS (MDIR=1, SEE FOLLOWING) ANGULAR DISTR.
     ARE DETERMINED AS FUNCTION OF ANGLE FROM AXIS PERPENDICULAR TO
     REACTION PLANE. NOTE THAT THIS IS ONLY PARTIAL INFORMATION.
     SHOULD MORE ANG. DIST. INFORMATION BE REQUIRED, NOTE THAT AT THE
     END THE VELOCITY VECTORS OF ALL FINAL PRODUCTS ARE SPECIFIED -
     VZ - PERPENDICULAR TO PLANE. VY - RECOIL DIRECTION. VX PERPENDICUL
     TO BOTH THESE.

 9)   A ROUTINE TO DETERMINE THE INPUT FUSION (COMPOUND NUCLEUS)
      CROSS SECTION VIA THE BASS METHOD (P.R.L. 1977) IS INCLUDED.


 10)  A NON-STATISTICAL YRAST CASCADE GAMMA DECAY CHAIN HAS BEEN
      ARTIFICIALLY INCORPORATED TO SIMULATE GAMMA MULTIPLICITY AND
      ENERGY RESULTS. WHEN THE LEVEL DENSITY TABLE CANNOT ACCOMODATE
      FURTHER DECAY DUE TO SPIN INHIBITION (DELTA-J .GT. 2 ) A CASCADE
      OF GAMMA RAYS WITH  DELTA-J = 2 AND  EGAMMA = EX *(4J-2)/ J(J+1)
      BRINGS THE INHIBITED CASCADE TO IT FINAL GROUND STATE.

 11)  A CASCADE DUMP AND TRACE FEATURE HAS BEEN ADDED TO  THE PROGRAM

      EVERY EMITTED PARTICLE IS DUMPED ON DISK (FILE 2) TOGETHER
      WITH ALL RELEVANT EMISSION PARAMETERS WHICH ENABLE A
      COMPLETE TRACEBACK DETERMINATION OF THE CASCADES WHICH LEAD
      TO FINAL SPECIFIC NUCLEI.THUS ONE CAN CORRELATE THE TYPE OF
      EMITTED PARTICLE,EXCITATION ENERGY, PARTICLE ENERGY, SPIN ETC.
      SEE DETAILED WORKING IN SUBROUTINE STATIS.

   *** THE TRACEBACK FEATURES ENABLE ONE TO CALCULATE VERY LOW
   *** FISSION PROBABILITIES THROUGH DUMPING FPROB, THE FISSION
   *** PROBABILITY AT EACH STAGE OF DEEXCITATION IN EACH CASCADE.
   *** THE SUM OF FISSION PROBABILITIES IS
   ***   SUM ( GAMMA-F/(GAMMA-N + GAMMA-F) )
   ***    ( SUM OVER ALL CASCADES).
   *** THIS VALUE IS DIFFERENT THAN THE EXACT VALUE WHICH CONTAINS
   ***        GAMMA-N / ( GAMMA-N + GAMMA-F ) OF THE PREVIOUS EVAP-
   *** -ORATION STAGE. THIS IS SOMEWHAT COMPENSATED BY THE FACT THAT
   *** IF THE FISSION PROBABILITY IS LARGE, THE ACTUAL FISSION IN
   *** THE MONTE-CARLO CALCULATION WILL DECREASE THE CALCULATED VALUE.
   ***
   *** IN ANY CASE , FOR HIGH FISSION PROBABILITY YOU SHOULD USE THE
   *** MONTE-CARLO VALUE AND NOT THE CALCULATED ONE.


 12) EXECUTION SPEED OF SUBROUTINE CHNPRB (WHERE THE PROGRAM SPENDS
     30 PERCENT OF ITS TIME) HAS BEEN IMPROVED SUBSTITUTING DO LOOPS
     IN PLACE OF IF'S.

     ADDITIONAL IMPORTANT MODIFICATION, FEBRUARY, 1982
     *************************************************

     THE INTERNAL LOOP OVER THE RUNNING SUM OF TL-S IN CHNPROB HAS
     BEEN REPLACED BY A DIFFERENCE OF SUMS IN A WAY WHICH IS RIGOROUS
     FOR SPINS OF 0 AND 1/2. THE OLD PART HAS BEEN COMMENTED OUT, SO
     ONE CAN STILL RESTORE IT IF ONE WANTS TO INCLUDE EVAPORATION OF
     PARTICLES WITH SPIN GREATER THAN 1/2 .

     THIS HAS REDUCED RUNNING TIME AGAIN BY 30 PERCENT

     *************************************************


      END MODIFICATION LIST ************************************

   THE STARTING NUCLEUS MAY BE  READ IN AS A COMPOUND NUCLEUS AT A
          CERTAIN EXCITATION ENERGY, IN WHICH CASE THE SPIN
          DISTRIBUTION IS ASSUMED TO BE THE EQUILIBRIUM
          DISTRIBUTION AS DETERMINED FROM THE LEVEL DENSITIES,
          OR IT MAY BE  READ IN AS A COMPOUND NUCLEUS
          WITH AN ASSIGNED SPIN DISTRIBUTION,
          OR IT MAY BE  READ IN AS A TARGET NUCLEUS AND A
          PROJECTILE OF A CERTAIN ENERGY, IN WHICH CASE THE
          THE EXCITATION ENERGY AND THE SPIN DISTRIBUTION ARE
          CALCULATED FROM THE OPTICAL MODEL.

   THE RESULTS OF THE CALCULATIONS ARE GIVEN AS DISTRIBUTIONS
          OF THE RESIDUAL NUCLEI.  ENERGY AND ANGULAR DISTRIBUTIONS OF
          THE EMITTED PARTICLES AND RECOIL ENERGY DISTRIBUTIONS
          OF THE RESIDUAL NUCLEI ARE ALSO AVAILABLE BY SUPPLYING
          AN APPROPRIATE CONTROL, AND ENERGY DISTRIBUTIONS OF GAMMAS
          EMITTED ARE ALSO AVAILABLE BY SUPPLYING AN APPROPRIATE
          CONTROL.
                 INPUT CARDS
*************************

     CARD 1.READ NCASC,IDIST,INPUT,FYRST,FISSBR,ARATIO,MDIR,FACLA,ITRAC
           , NOSHL
     *******
              FORMAT(3I5,3F5.0,I5,F5.,2I5)
  NCASC - NUMBER OF CASCADES. (EVENTS IN MONTE CARLO CALCULATION)
  IDIST -  =1  DETAILED ANGULAR AND ENERGY DISTRIBUTION OF RESIDUAL
               NUCLEI AND EVAPORATED PARTICLES.
           =0  BRIEF, SCHEMATIC RESULTS OF PARTICLE SPECTRA AND LIST
               OF EVAPORATED (RESIDUAL) NUCLEI POPULATION.
  INPUT -  =1  PROJECTILE + TARGET INPUT . FIRST OPTICAL POTENTIAL
               (SEE BELOW) MUST BE FOR THE PROCTILE-TARGET COMBINATION.
           =2  COMPOUND NUCLEUS INPUT FOR SINGLE SPIN.
           =3  COMPOUND NUCLEUS INPUT. SPIN DISTRIBUTION READ IN.
           =4  COMPOUND NUCLEUS INPUT. SPIN DISTRIBUTION CALCULATED
               TAKING SPIN-CUTOFF PARAMATER AT GIVEN EXCITATION ENERGY.
               (FROM GILBERT AND CAMERON).
           =5  TRIANGULAR  (SIGMA = 2L+1 ) CROSS SECTION BETWEEN
                LMINN AND MAXIMUM SPIN
        INPUT CAN ALSO BE EQUAL TO MINUS THESE VALUES.
        IN THIS CASE, THE CASCADE DUMP OR DETAILED TRACE OF
        THE EMISSION POINTS IN THE E-J PLANE OF ALL PARTICLES
         LEADING TO A GIVEN FINAL PRODUCT IS SUPRESSED.
         FOR THIS,  FT02F001 (TAPE2) SHOULD BE DEFINED ACCORDINGLY.
         (SEE JCL SECTION)
  FYRST  -  PARAMETER DETERMINING YRAST LINE TO BE USED.
           =0.  GILBERT-CAMERON SPIN CUTOFF PARAMETER.
                EROT = (SPIN)**2/(2.*SIGSQ)
       N E. 0.  EROT = ROTATING LIQUID DROP ROTATIONAL ENERGY,
                MULTIPLIED BY FACTOR OF FYRST.
            IN BOTH CASES LEVEL DENSITY CALCULATED AT E = EX-EROT.
  FISSBR  -  THE PROGRAM ASSUMES THE COHEN-PLASIL-SWIATECKI ROTATING
             LIQUID DROP BARRIER IF THIS IS LEFT BLANK. IF YOU PROVIDE
             A FISSION BARRIER OF YOUR OWN, THE C-P-S BARRIER WILL BE
             RENORMALIZED ACCORDINGLY.
             IF FISSBR IS POSITIVE IT WILL BE TAKEN AS THE DESIRED
             ZERO SPIN FISSION BARRIER.
             IF FISSBR IS NEGATIVE, ITS ABSOLUTE VALUE WILL BE TAKEN
             AS A FACTOR TO MULTIPLY THE C.P.S. BARRIER.
  ARATIO  -  RATIO OF THE FERMI GAS LEVEL DENSITY PARAMETER 'LITTLE A'
             AT THE SADDLE POINT TO THE GROUND STATE VALUE.LEAVE BLANK
             IF YOU WANT ARATIO = 1.000. THE SADDLE POINT LEVEL DENS-
             -ITY IS DETERMINED BY G.S. 'LITTLE-A' X ARATIO
  *** THIS IS MAJOR CHANGE *** 10/15/81
  *** PREVIOUSLY FISSION LEVEL DENSITY CALCULATED IN MORE APPROXIMATE
  *** MANNER. FISSION PROBABILITY CHANGE OF 20 PERCENT UPWARD RESULTS.
  MDIR    -  = 0 . COMPOUND NUCLEUS IS INITIALLY IN  M=0 STATES AND
                   THE  Z-AXIS IS THE RECOIL AXIS.
             = 1 . COMPOUND NUCLEUS IS INITIALLY IN  M=J STATES, THE
                   Z AXIS IS PERPENDICULAR TO RECOIL DIRECTION (AND
                   REACTION PLANE). THIS CALCULATION IS APPROPRIATE FOR
                   DEEP INELASTIC FRAGMENT DEEXCITATION.
  FACLA  -        LEVEL DENSITY PARAMETER = MASS/FACLA IF NOT ZERO.
                  OTHERWISE GILBERT AND CAMERON LITTLE-A TAKEN.
  ITRAC -  THIS CARD CONTROLS THE DEGREE OF EVENT TRACEBACK IF YOU HAVE
           OPTED FOR IT.( I.E. 'INPUT' IS POSITIVE)

           = 0 PRODUCES COMPACT TRACEBACK, SUMMED OVER ALL RESIDUES
           = 1 DETAILED TRACEBACK LEADING TO EACH INDIVIDUAL ISOTOPE
               SEPARATELY
           *** REMEMBER, TO SUPRESS TRACEBACK COMPLETELY,USE NEGATIVE
           *** VALUE OF 'INPUT'
  NOSHL - =0 USES REGULAR (WAPSTRA) MASS TABLE OFF DISK
          =1 USES LYSEKIL MASSES WITH SHELL CORRECTION ZEROED
             (THIS IS BELIEVED TO BE APPROPRIATE FOR HIGH EX WHEN
             SHELL EFFECTS HAVE WASHED OUT ).

  END CARD 1

  CARD 2
  *********

      THERE ARE 2 POSSIBILITIES . INPUT = 1, OR INPUT GT 1
  INPUT=1
        READ IZP,IAP,IZT,IAT,SP,ST,QCN
        FORMAT(4I5,2F5.0,2F10.0)
  IZP,IAP  -  Z AND A OF PROJECTILE
  IZT,IAT  -  Z AND A OF TARGET
  SP, ST   -  GROUND STATE SPINS OF PROJECTILE AND TARGET
  QCN      -  Q VALUE OF REACTION.
          -  IF LEFT ZERO IT IS CALCULATED FROM MASS TABLES.

  FOR INPUT =1, AN ADDITIONAL CARD 2A IS NECESSARY
  CARD 2A  (IF(INPUT.EQ.1))
  *******
         READ ELAB,EXPSIG,JCMAX,AGRAZ,ELOSS,LMINN
         FORMAT(2F10.5,I5,2F5.0,I5)
  ELAB  -  BEAM ENERGY
  EXPSIG-  EXPERIMENTAL FUSION CROSS SECTION IF KNOWN. TL-S FROM
           OPTICAL MODEL SHIFTED TO REPRODUCE THIS VALUE IF INPUTTED,
           PRESERVING THE L-DIFFUSENESS.
  ******** EXPSIG = 0 RESULTS IN BASS MODEL(P.R.L. 1977) FUSION
           CROSS SECTION BEING USED.
  JCMAX -  MAXIMUM J TO BE USED DURING CALCULATIONS. (IF 0 IT IS
           TAKEN FROM OPTICAL MODEL ROUTINE.
  AGRAZ -  TO BYPASS INPUT CHANNEL OPTICAL MODEL ROUTINE (TLOM)
           SPECIFY L - DIFFUSENESS OF FUSION CROSS SECTION
           IF AGRAZ EQ 0. DIFFUSENESS WILL BE DETERMINED BY REACTION
           CROSS SECTION  DIFFUSENESS
  ELOSS -  ENERGY LOSS OF BEAM THRU FULL TARGET WIDTH. ( TOTAL DE )
           ENERGIES WILL BE DISTRIBUTED BETWEEN EBEAM & EBEAM-ELOSS
  LMINN    LOWEST PARTIAL WAVE L IN CALCULATION.
           PARTIAL WAVES FROM L=0 TO LMINN EXCLUDED, ENABLING LOW-L
           NON-FUSION WINDOW IN REACTION CALCULATION.

     ***** NOTE - IF YOU ARE RUNNING AT HIGH BOMBARDING ENERGIES
      FOR WHICH THE GRAZING ANGULAR MOMENTUM IS ABOVE 75 HBAR,
      IT IS RECOMENDED TO INPUT  AGRAZ GT 0, AND TO SPECIFY
      AN ARBITRARY VALUE FOR EXPSIG (OR 0 = BASS) WHICH CORRESPONDS TO
      A FUSION CROSS SECTION WITH A LIMITING L-VALUE AROUND 80.
      THIS WILL GIVE YOU ALL THE EVAPORATION RESIDUE DATA AND THE
      FISSION PROBABILITIES YOU NEED. FOR J GT 80  ALL NUCLEI WILL
      FISSION ANYWAY, AND YOU WILL RUN OUT OF DIMENSION IF YOU TRY.

  CARD 2  INPUT=2, 3, 4, OR 5
  ******
          *******
          READ IZ,IA,EEXCN,EREC,AJNUC,LMINN
          FORMAT(2I5,3F10.0,I5)
  IZ,IA,EEXCN - Z, A, EXCITATION ENERGY OF COMPOUND NUCLEUS
  EREC,AJNUC  - RECOIL ENERGY AND MAXIMUM SPIN OF COMPOUND NUCLEUS
  LMINN       - LOWEST PARTIAL WAVE IN CALCULATION
                ( SEE ABOVE )
  IF INPUT=3, AN ADDITIONAL CARD 2A IS NECESSARY
  CARD 2A (IF INPUT=3)
  *******
          READ MAXC,(SIGJC(I),I=1,MAXC)
          FORMAT(I5,5X,7F10.5/(8F10.5))
  MAXC  - MAXIMUM SPIN INDEX (I.E. J+1 FOR EVEN NUCLEUS, J+0.5 FOR
          ODD NUCLEUS
  SIGJC - PARTIAL CROSS SECTION FOR SPECIFIC J

  END CARD 2  (AND 2A FOR INPUT=1 AND INPUT=3)

   OPTICAL MODEL POTENTIALS ARE  READ IN

 1) INCOMING CHANNEL. 2)NEUTRON. 3)PROTON. 4)ALPHA.

   CARDS 3A,3B,3C,3D (INPUT=1), CARDS 3A,3B,3C (INPUT=2,3,4)
   *****************
   EACH CARD IS A COMPLETE SET OF POTENTIAL PARAMETERS.
      READ V0D(I),V01D(I),V02D(I),R0RD(I),ARD(I),R0CD(I),W0D(I),
    1W01D(I),W02D(I),R0ID(I),AID(I),RMCHD(I),NPD(I),IMAG(I),IRAD(I)
     FORMAT(12F5.0,I5,2I3)
   DEPTH OF REAL NUCLEAR WELL IS GIVEN BY
          V0=V0D(I)+V01D(I)*ECM+V02D(I)*ECM**2
   DEPTH OF IMAGINARY NUCLEAR WELL IS GIVEN BY
          W0=W0D(I)+W01D(I)*ECM+W02D(I)*ECM**2
   IF IMAG(I)=1 IMAGINARY WELL IS SURFACE POTENTIAL
          OTHERWISE IT IS A VOLUME POTENTIAL
   RADIAL EXTENSION OF NUCLEAR WELL
          IF IRAD(I)=0  RADIUS OF REAL NUCLEAR WELL IS R0RD(I)
                        RADIUS OF IMAGINARY NUCLEAR WELL IS R0ID(I)
          IF IRAD(I)=1  RADIUS OF REAL WELL IS R0RD(I)*AMT**(1./3.)
                        RADIUS OF IMAGINARY WELL IS ROID(I)*AMT**(1/3)
          IF IRAD(I)=2  RADIUS OF REAL WELL IS
                               R0RD(I)*(AMT**(1/3)+AMP(1/3))
                        RADIUS OF IMAGINARY WELL IS
                               R0ID(I)*(AMT**(1/3)+AMP**(1/3))
   RADIUS OF COULOMB POTENTIAL IS R0CD(I) AND IS CONTROLLED BY IRAD(I)
